A working note for Ally and Tom

A read on where your concierge layer actually holds up, and where it starts to eat the week.

You built Klerk to fix something the big TMCs never touched: corporate rates for SMBs, flexible event housing without room blocks, and attendees who feel looked after. The site says "Event Travel on Autopilot." The Get Started flow says "schedule a demo with one of our team members." Both are true. The autopilot is running, and somebody is also always on the phone. That gap is where we work.

The frame

Most of what your team does by hand is not an AI problem.

About 60% is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. The real AI slice, maybe 10%, is where a Klerk-trained assistant can actually talk to an attendee or an organizer the way your concierge team does. Most shops miss this split and put an LLM on problems that Postgres solves faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees. We help Klerk put each piece on the right layer.

60%
Traditional code

Rate lookups, itinerary updates, hotel partner onboarding.

30%
Rule-based logic

Deposit thresholds, cancellation windows, supplier-specific exceptions.

10%
Genuine AI

Concierge-grade conversation with organizers and attendees, in your voice.

The case
Feeld, CTO engagement

A scoped sprint with a product CTO who needed one workflow to hold up without him.

We ran a scoped sprint with the CTO at Feeld. Same profile: product company, founder-led, CTO choosing which internal workflow to harden before the team outgrows tribal knowledge. We delivered a scoped Organizational Context Architecture and a Strategic Operations Framework, both of which the Feeld team kept running after the engagement ended. The goal was never a demo. The goal was a workflow that kept working when the founders stopped touching it.

The paper

One relevant piece of writing.

Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM TiiS. It explains how to encode operational judgment as structured context so an agent can run a process the way your senior operator would.

Context on the bench behind this work: Jake also built an online community to 22,000 members in 5 weeks. Eduba partners with NLP Logix for work that sits below the orchestration layer; NLP Logix has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists.
